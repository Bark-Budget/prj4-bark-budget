Affordable Dog Food

Do you want to spice things up a bit? Dog food can be really expensive. That is why we have put together this cost-reducing do-it-yourself for your dog’s food. Good to know: this recipe is vet-approved and has all the necessary nutrition. Not only is this recipe cheaper than most brands of dogfood in the pet store, it is also fun to make and with a little bit of luck your dog will love you for it!

    • Grooming Hacks

      Most dogs love to go outside to play. Unfortunately, most of the time they come back muddy, dirty and wet. Not only do you want to clean their little paws to keep your house clean, but it is also very important for their health. However, it doesn't necessarily have to be expensive. Most of the paw cleaning products we found were between $10 and $20. Which is quite ridiculous when you can make the paw cleaner yourself! Scroll down to find our DIY recipe for the paw cleaner! If your dog doesn't like their paws cleaned, you could try to put some peanutbutter on your shoulder (but plastic foil in between) and they might sit still!


      What you'll need:

    • Dish soap (€1.19 at Jumbo, Aldi etc.)
    • Hydrogen peroxide (€1.25 at Kruidvat, Etos, etc.)
    • Coconut oil (€2.50 at Jumbo, Aldi)
    • Spray bottle (€1.11 at Action or better yet, a free reused one from home)

      • How to:

      1. Add 3 cups of water into a large mixing bowl

      2. Add in the dish soap, coconut oil, and peroxide. Whisk until well combined

      3. Put the mixture in the spray bottle Spray your dogs paws on the bottom and top to disinfectant. You could clean it with a soft toothpbrush.
